    01 • Tone & Voice

    The difference between voice and tone is that a voice is how we speak to others, much like any person's unique voice and style. A tone is how we project our feeling at any given time. Tone can also change depending on the circumstance, environment, or the party you are talking to.

    Tone

    The language should be pretty informal and friendly, as if you were talking to a good friend, genuinely trying to help them through a problem. Never belittle or catch the user off guard with anything. At all times, when choosing between being entertaining and being transparent, always go with the latter.

    Be mindful that the tone can change depending on the stage of the user journey through the app experience. For example, you can have an informal tone at the start of a user's journey – casual browsing and information gathering.

    You can be a bit more formal when booking an appointment with an agent, confirming details, or reminding users of necessary upcoming meetings.

    Technical errors, such as server lags, crashes, or input errors, should be handled carefully and tactfully. Be friendly and let the user down gently!

    Voice

    We go out of our way to help the user find the best solution for them in the least amount of time and effort. We remember details (from their profiles) and present relevant information. At all times, we should always adopt a mentality of service first.

    Our writing style is upbeat at all times. We make finding a home more accessible and empower others to do it independently with the best possible information available for every property.

    When we make suggestions, we remember how many places they’ve seen, bookmarked, inquired about, and suggest relevant results.


    02 • Colors

    The primary color for the brand is Deep Blue. The accent color is a Gold Yellow. Together these colors invoke a feeling of trust and groundedness, with a hint of prestige and happiness.

    Several shades of gray differentiate body text from inactive text and provide subtle hints of visual hierarchy.

    These colors are used throughout the mobile and web app to ground the experience while calling specific attention to items of importance. These include confirmation dialogue boxes or calls to action such as signing in and out.

    Primary & Secondary Colors

    A monochromatic scheme allows for varying shades of the primary blue color, providing subtle contrast and strengthening the visual hierarchy of elements on the screen.

    03 • Typography

    The typeface for the brand is made up of 3 typefaces, Metropolis, Source Sans Pro, and Open Sans. Each has it’s purpose and are all sans-serif to maintain a modern look and feel while maximizing readability.

    Typefaces

    Metropolis

    Clean and classy, the bold weighted sans-serif font. The geometric shape and minimalistic design invoke a modern, sleek, and somewhat futuristic feeling. This aligns with the vision for the app to be clean and bright.

    Metropolis is also used for menus in an extra light weight to contrast with the main headers on the screen. The kerning values for the menu type increases slightly to provide visual interest for the animated main menu.

    Source Sans Pro

    This highly readable font has a large x-height values. Aperture values are bigger lending itself to readability in various weights and sizes. The typeface gives off a feeling of simplicity with a modern touch.

    Open Sans

    This typeface works well with numbers because of lining and tabular figures. Spacing of numbers, punctuation, and common symbols are consistent, making the numbers readable.

    This font is also used for button text, because of consistent styling and readability at small font size, all caps, and bold weight.
